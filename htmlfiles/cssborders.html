My Favourite Places

Israel

Israel's history dates back to biblical times, with the Hebrew patriarchs and the establishment of a united monarchy under Kings Saul, David, and Solomon. The kingdom later split, and both Israel and Judah fell to foreign empires, leading to the Babylonian exile in 586 BCE. The Jews returned under Persian rule and rebuilt the Second Temple, but Roman rule led to its destruction in 70 CE and Jewish dispersal. Muslim Arabs conquered the region in 636 CE, followed by Crusader, Mamluk, and Ottoman rule. The Zionist movement emerged in the late 19th century, advocating for a Jewish homeland in Palestine. After World War I, Britain controlled the region, and tensions grew between Jews and Arabs. In 1948, Israel declared independence, sparking the first Arab-Israeli war. Israel fought multiple wars, including the Six-Day War (1967) and Yom Kippur War (1973), shaping its modern borders. Peace efforts, such as the Egypt-Israel Treaty (1979) and Oslo Accords (1993), sought conflict resolution. Today, Israel remains a key player in Middle Eastern politics, balancing security, diplomacy, and regional tensions.

Switzerland

The Alpine country of Switzerland is globally renowned for its spectacular landscapes. It is often regarded as the most beautiful country in Europe. Switzerland is the birthplace of many inventions like the first solar-powered airplane and milk chocolates.Switzerland,[d] officially the Swiss Confederation,[e] is a landlocked country located in west-central Europe.[f][13] It is bordered by Italy to the south, France to the west, Germany to the north, and Austria and Liechtenstein to the east. Switzerland is geographically divided among the Swiss Plateau, the Alps and the Jura; the Alps occupy the greater part of the territory, whereas most of the country's nearly 9 million people are concentrated on the plateau, which hosts its largest cities and economic centres, including Zurich, Geneva, and Lausanne.

Paris

The City of Paris is the centre of the Île-de-France region, or Paris Region, with an official estimated population of 12,271,794 inhabitants in January 2023, or about 19% of the population of France.The Paris Region had a nominal GDP of €765 billion (US$1.064 trillion when adjusted for PPP) in 2021, the highest in the European Union. According to the Economist Intelligence Unit Worldwide Cost of Living Survey, in 2022, Paris was the city with the ninth-highest cost of living in the world. Paris is a major railway, highway, and air-transport hub served by two international airports: Charles de Gaulle Airport, the third-busiest airport in Europe, and Orly Airport.Opened in 1900, the city's subway system, the Paris Métro, serves 5.23 million passengers daily.It is the second-busiest metro system in Europe after the Moscow Metro

DUBAI

Dubai, one of the seven emirates of the United Arab Emirates (UAE), has transformed from a small fishing village into a global hub for business, tourism, and innovation. Established as a trading port in the early 19th century, Dubai’s rapid development accelerated after the discovery of oil in the 1960s. However, the city strategically diversified its economy, focusing on trade, real estate, finance, and tourism. Iconic landmarks such as the Burj Khalifa, the world’s tallest building, and the Palm Jumeirah, an artificial island, showcase its architectural ambitions. Dubai is also home to luxury shopping malls, high-end resorts, and attractions like the Dubai Mall and the Burj Al Arab. The emirate hosts major global events, including Expo 2020 and the Dubai Shopping Festival, attracting millions of visitors annually. Its strategic location makes it a major transportation hub, with Dubai International Airport being one of the busiest in the world. The city’s business-friendly policies and tax incentives have made it a financial powerhouse, attracting multinational corporations and investors. While modern and cosmopolitan, Dubai preserves its cultural heritage through traditional souks, museums, and historic districts. Today, it continues to evolve as a futuristic city, embracing technology, sustainability, and innovation.
